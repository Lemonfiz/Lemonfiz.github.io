What the fuck is an ATS?

I moved to San Diego with the hopes of becoming a great hiring consultant that could bring human resources into the new century. Upon arrival my hopes of finding a secure job quickly were dashed, and I also found that the industry needed more help than I realized.

Every Interview I attended had one thing in common. They wanted to know my experience with applicant tracking systems (ATS), Vendor management systems (VMS), CRM’s, etc. The only ATS I had ever worked on was a small CSU hosted one when I worked as a recruitment assistant doing some hiring. I never learned about any of these systems in any classes or books or talking to professors, yet these companies held up ATS as the grand solution to hiring.

Now that I have looked at some ATS I will admit they are helpful, and they all essentially serve the same purpose to track applicants, which previously was done with paper and tables. ATS fluency is treated as complicated as language fluency yet I can’t major in ADP workforce or BambooHR.
